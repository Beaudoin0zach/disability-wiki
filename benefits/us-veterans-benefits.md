# Veterans’ Disability Benefits (U.S.)

Disabled veterans in the United States may qualify for benefits through the **Department of Veterans Affairs (VA)** in addition to, or instead of, Social Security and other programs.

This page gives a basic map and invites more detailed contributions from veterans and advocates.

---

## Major VA Disability-Related Programs

### VA Disability Compensation

- Tax-free monthly benefit for veterans with **service-connected disabilities** (conditions caused or worsened by military service).  
- Amount is based on a **disability rating** (percentage) and, in some cases, dependents.  
- Ratings can be combined and can change over time.

---

### VA Pension (Needs-Based)

- Income-based benefit for some wartime veterans with limited income and assets, and who are older or disabled.  
- Rules differ from VA disability compensation and from SSI/SSDI.

---

### VA Health Care

- Access to VA medical centers and clinics, often with **priority groups** based on disability rating, income, and other factors.  
- May include mental health services, prosthetics, rehabilitation, and specialized disability care.

---

### Other VA-Related Supports

Depending on circumstances, veterans may also have access to:

- **Caregiver support programs**  
- **Education benefits**  
- **Housing adaptations and grants**  
- Vocational rehabilitation and employment services  
- Transportation assistance and travel reimbursement for medical appointments

---

## Interaction with Social Security and Other Programs

Veterans can often:

- Receive **VA disability compensation and SSDI/SSI at the same time**, though each program has its own rules.  
- Use **VA health care** alongside **Medicare** or **Medicaid**, depending on eligibility.  
- Be impacted by VA pension rules if they also receive other income.

Understanding how these programs interact can be complicated. Many veterans work with:

- **Veterans Service Organizations (VSOs)**  
- Accredited VA representatives  
- Legal aid or specialized disability attorneys

---

## Applying and Appealing

VA disability and pension claims:

- Are filed through the VA (online, by mail, or with help from a representative).  
- Require evidence of **service connection**, medical records, and other documentation.  
- Can be appealed through multiple levels if denied or underrated.
