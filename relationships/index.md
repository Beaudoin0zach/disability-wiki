# Relationships, Family, and Care

Disabled people form friendships, families, and communities in all kinds of ways.

This section covers:

- **Dating and Relationships** – Intimacy, consent, online dating, and ableism in romance.
- **Sexuality and Reproductive Health** – Access to information, contraception, pregnancy, abortion, and sterilization histories.
- **Parenting with a Disability** – Rights, supports, and common prejudices.
- **Parents of Disabled Children** – Navigating systems without erasing your child’s autonomy.
- **Caregiving** – Family, friend, and paid caregiving dynamics.
- **Abuse, Safety, and Consent** – Overlaps with the Crisis section.

We emphasize **mutuality and consent**, not hero narratives or “burden” framing.
