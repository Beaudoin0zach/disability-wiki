# Welcome to DisabilityWiki

You've found a community-built resource for disabled people, by disabled people.

This page explains what you're looking at and why this wiki exists.

## Historical Origins and Movement Leadership

DisabilityWiki grew from a simple question: **Why is disability information scattered across dozens of websites, government portals, and organizations?**

Disabled people—especially those in the Global South, Indigenous communities, and multiply-marginalized folks—have long created our own knowledge systems. We share strategies in online communities, pass down wisdom through peer support, organize collectively for our rights. But this knowledge rarely gets centralized or treated as expertise.

In 2024–2025, disabled activists, advocates, and peer support specialists came together to build this wiki. We believed that disability knowledge should be:
- **Free and open** to everyone
- **Led by disabled people** (not about us without us)
- **Comprehensive globally** (not just Western-centric)
- **Practical and actionable** (not just theoretical)
- **Accessible to all disabled people** (including those with disabilities that make reading hard)

This wiki carries forward a long tradition of disabled people building community knowledge and mutual aid.

## What This Wiki Is

DisabilityWiki is a **free, open-source knowledge base** about:
- Your legal rights as a disabled person
- How to access benefits, healthcare, and housing
- How to navigate education and employment
- Assistive technology and accessibility tools
- How disability intersects with other identities
- Resources for peer support and community
- Disability history, culture, and activism

It's organized by topic (not by disability type, not by country first—though you can search by those too).

**Example:** Looking for information about housing accessibility? You'll find:
- What your legal rights are (Fair Housing Act, local laws)
- How to find accessible housing
- A checklist to evaluate a home
- Information on housing programs by country
- How different disabilities affect housing needs
- Stories and strategies from other disabled people

## What This Wiki Is NOT

- **Not a substitute for medical advice** — Always consult doctors and healthcare providers
- **Not a substitute for legal advice** — For legal situations, see a lawyer or legal aid organization
- **Not exhaustive** — We're always expanding and improving
- **Not the only resource** — Use this alongside disability organizations, peer networks, and professional support
- **Not a diagnosis tool** — We don't diagnose disabilities

## Why It Exists: Our Mission

We built this wiki because disabled people deserve:

**Information without gatekeeping.** Disability knowledge shouldn't be locked behind medical credentials or expensive services. Disabled people are experts in disability.

**Global representation.** Most disability resources center the Global North (US, Europe, Canada, Australia). This wiki actively centers the Global South, Indigenous frameworks, and multiply-marginalized voices.

**Dignity.** Disability resources often use charity language ("help the disabled") or inspiration narratives. We reject that. This wiki centers disabled people's agency, power, and self-determination.

**Community leadership.** This wiki is built by disabled people, for disabled people. Allies are welcome, but disabled people decide what gets included and how it's framed.

**Accessibility.** This wiki works for screen readers, mobile phones, and multiple ways of reading and understanding information.

## How This Wiki Is Organized

The wiki is organized by **topic area**, not by disability type or country. Here are the main sections:

- **Your Rights & Laws** — Legal protections and how to advocate for yourself
- **Money & Benefits** — Financial support programs
- **Health & Care** — Healthcare, mental health, caregiving
- **Housing** — Finding accessible homes, rights, programs
- **Education** — K–12, college, accommodations
- **Work & Employment** — Jobs, accommodations, rights
- **Assistive Technology** — Tools and equipment
- **Specific Disabilities** — Information about particular disabilities
- **Intersectionality** — How disability intersects with race, gender, immigration, etc.
- **Community & Activism** — Peer support, organizing, advocacy

Within each section, you'll find pages about **different countries, regions, and approaches**. So "Housing" includes:
- Legal frameworks (US Fair Housing Act, Australian law, etc.)
- Practical guides (how to search, what to look for)
- Programs (Section 8, public housing, etc.)
- Global examples (informal housing in Global South, etc.)

## How to Use This Wiki

**Option 1: Search** — Use the search box at the top to find keywords  
**Option 2: Browse by topic** — Click on a section above  
**Option 3: Follow related pages** — Each page links to related content  

Every page follows the same structure, so you always know where to find information:

1. **Opening summary** — What the page is about
2. **Historical context** — Where this came from, who fought for it
3. **Key information** — The main content
4. **Why it matters** — Why this is important for disabled people
5. **Strengths** — What works well
6. **Challenges** — Where it falls short
7. **Different disabilities** — How it applies to different people
8. **How to use it** — Practical strategies for advocates
9. **Global perspectives** — How it works in different parts of the world
10. **Resources** — Links and contact info

## Our Values: Disability Justice

This wiki is built on **disability justice principles**:

**Nothing About Us Without Us.** Disabled people lead all decisions. We don't let non-disabled people speak for us.

**Intersectionality.** Disability doesn't exist in isolation. We center multiply-marginalized people (disabled people of color, disabled trans people, disabled people in the Global South, etc.).

**Identity-first language.** We say "disabled people," not "people with disabilities." This centers disability as an identity, not as tragedy or loss.

**Interdependence over independence.** We reject the "independence at all costs" model. Disabled people are interdependent—we rely on each other and that's beautiful and powerful.

**Leadership of most-impacted.** The people most affected by a policy or issue should lead solutions. This means Global South disabled people lead Global South content, disabled trans people lead LGBTQ+ disability content, etc.

**Collective power.** Individual accommodation isn't enough. We need systemic change. This wiki emphasizes collective action, mutual aid, and movement building.

## Who Built This Wiki?

Ideally, this wiki will be created by disabled volunteers—peer support specialists, advocates, organizers, students, and more. We're still building and we need more contributors, especially from the Global South and most-marginalized communities.

## Accessibility Features of This Wiki

This wiki is built for disabled people to use:

- **Screen reader compatible** — Semantic structure works with assistive technology
- **Mobile friendly** — Works on phones and tablets
- **Plain language** — We explain concepts clearly
- **Keyboard navigation** — No mouse required
- **No autoplay media** — Videos and audio need your permission
- **High contrast** — Text is readable
- **Resizable text** — Adjust font size to your needs
- **Growing translation support** — Gradually adding multiple languages

Find problems? See [[/about/accessibility|Accessibility Statement]] for how to report issues.

## How to Get the Most From This Wiki

**Take your time.** This is a lot of information. You don't need to read everything at once.

**Start where you are.** If you need housing info, go there first. You don't need to read everything in order.

**Ask for help.** If something is confusing, let us know. We want this to be clear.

**Share with others.** Know someone who needs this info? Pass it along.

**Come back.** This wiki grows constantly. Check back for updates.
