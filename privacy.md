*Last updated: July 23, 2026*

Disability Wiki is built on a simple principle: looking up disability rights,
benefits, or a crisis hotline should never cost you your privacy.

## What we collect

**Nothing.** Disability Wiki does not collect, store, or transmit any personal
information about you — on the website or in the app. There are:

- **No accounts** — you never sign in, and we never ask for your name, email, or
  phone number to read anything.
- **No tracking** — no analytics, no advertising, no third-party trackers, no
  device fingerprinting.
- **No permissions** — the app does not request access to your location, contacts,
  camera, microphone, or any other device data.

## How the app uses the network

The Disability Wiki app works fully offline. It connects to the internet only to:

- **Load the latest reference content** when you have a connection, and
- **Download content updates** — the app periodically fetches updated pages (for
  example, a corrected crisis hotline number) from this website.

These are ordinary, one-way downloads of public information. The app sends no
information about you when it does this. Content updates are cryptographically
signed, and the app installs them only if the signature is valid.

## Phone numbers and links

When you tap a phone number — a crisis hotline, for instance — your device's phone
app opens with the number ready to dial. You always choose whether to place the
call. When you tap a link to an outside website, it opens in your browser, and that
website's own privacy policy applies.

## Children

We collect no data from anyone, including children.
