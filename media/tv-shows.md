## Essential Viewing

### Groundbreaking Representation

- ✦ 🎭 **Switched at Birth** (2011-2017) – ABC Family/Freeform. Drama centering two families, one with a Deaf daughter. Features extensive ASL, Deaf actors (Katie Leclerc, Marlee Matlin, Sean Berdy), and storylines about Deaf culture, cochlear implant debates, and Deaf education. Landmark for Deaf representation on mainstream TV.

- ✦ 🎭 **Deaf U** (2020) – Netflix. Reality series following Deaf and hard of hearing students at Gallaudet University. All Deaf cast. Explores dating, identity, Deaf culture, and the diversity within Deaf community.

- ✦ 🎭 🎬 **Special** (2019-2021) – Netflix. Ryan O'Connell (gay man with cerebral palsy) created, wrote, and stars in this comedy about a gay man with CP navigating life, work, and relationships. Groundbreaking for authentic disabled creator telling own story.

- ✦ 🎭 **Speechless** (2016-2019) – ABC. Comedy about family with teenager (Micah Fowler, who has CP) who uses AAC device. Fowler is authentically cast; show consulted disability community. Rare positive portrayal of AAC user as full character.

## Classic & Influential Series

### 1980s-1990s

- **Life Goes On** (1989-1993) – ABC. Chris Burke, actor with Down syndrome, starred as Corky. First series with main character with Down syndrome. Groundbreaking for era.

- **Reasonable Doubts** (1991-1993) – NBC. Marlee Matlin as Deaf assistant DA. Early mainstream Deaf representation.

- 🎭 **Facts of Life** (1979-1988) – Geri Jewell, actress with cerebral palsy, had recurring role as Cousin Geri. First actor with CP in recurring primetime role.

### 2000s

- **Joan of Arcadia** (2003-2005) – CBS. Featured characters with disabilities; addressed chronic illness.

- **House** (2004-2012) – Fox. Hugh Laurie as doctor with chronic pain, uses cane. [Note: Disability often used as character flaw rather than identity]

- **Breaking Bad** (2008-2013) – AMC. RJ Mitte, actor with CP, plays Walter Jr. who has CP. Authentically cast supporting character.

### 2010s

- ✦ 🎭 **Switched at Birth** (2011-2017) – [See Essential Viewing]

- ✦ 🎭 **Speechless** (2016-2019) – [See Essential Viewing]

- **Glee** (2009-2015) – Fox. Kevin McHale played wheelchair-using Artie. [Note: Non-disabled actor in wheelchair; criticized. But also: featured Ali Stroker, disabled actress, and Lauren Potter, actress with Down syndrome]

- **Parenthood** (2010-2015) – NBC. Max Braverman, autistic character central to family drama. [Note: Non-autistic actor; but consulted extensively]

- **The OA** (2016-2019) – Netflix. Brit Marling as woman with NDE and possible psychiatric experiences; featured blind character.

## Problematic but Notable

Shows that achieved visibility but have significant representation issues.

- **Atypical** (2017-2021) – Netflix. Non-autistic actor in lead; criticized for stereotypes. Later seasons added autistic writers and actors.

- **The Good Doctor** (2017-present) – Non-autistic actor; savant tropes. Popular but mixed community reception.

- **Glee** (2009-2015) – Non-disabled actor in wheelchair. But also featured genuinely disabled actors in other roles.

- **Me Before You** (2016) – Film, but so widely discussed. [Warning: Disability as fate worse than death trope]

- **House** (2004-2012) – Chronic pain portrayed as character flaw, addiction normalized negatively.

## Behind the Scenes

### Disabled Showrunners & Writers

- 🎬 **Ryan O'Connell** – Special (creator, writer, star)

- 🎬 **Josh Feldman** – This Close (co-creator, star) – Deaf
- 🎬 **Grainne McGuinness** – Pablo (creator) – autistic

### Writers' Rooms with Disabled Writers

- Speechless – Consulted disability community; hired disabled writers
- Atypical – Added autistic writers in later seasons after criticism
- As We See It – Included autistic perspectives in development

### Casting Directors Prioritizing Authentic Casting

Growing industry movement toward authentic casting. Organizations like RespectAbility advocate for disabled actors.
