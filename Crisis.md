# Crisis Help: Immediate Support When You Need It

If you're in crisis right now, you don't have to face it alone.

This section provides fast access to crisis support, emergency resources, and safety planning tools.

---

## ⚠️ IN IMMEDIATE DANGER?

**Life-threatening emergency:**
- **Call 911** (US/Canada)
- **Call 999** (UK)
- **Call 000** (Australia)
- **Call 112** (Europe, many countries)
- **Go to nearest emergency room**

**Don't wait.** Emergency services exist for this.

## Beyond Crisis: Safety Planning & Support

### Create an Emergency Plan

Preparing now helps when you're in crisis and can't think clearly.

**[[Emergency Preparedness|Emergency Preparedness Guide]] includes:**

- Who to contact (trusted people in your life)
- What helps you (activities, coping strategies)
- Crisis hotline numbers to save
- Safety planning worksheet
- Where to go if not safe

---

### If You're Helping Someone Else

If someone tells you they're suicidal or in crisis:

- **Listen without judgment** — Take it seriously
- **Don't minimize** — Don't say "you'll be fine" or "others have it worse"
- **Ask directly** — "Are you thinking about suicide?" (asking doesn't cause it)
- **Help them access resources** — Call a crisis line together
- **Stay with them if possible** — Don't leave them alone
- **Call 911 if immediate danger** — Their safety matters more than their anger
- **You're not responsible for fixing it** — Your job is to support and connect them to help

**You're not alone in this:** Crisis lines can help supporters too.

## Important Things to Know

### All These Services Are FREE

No cost. No insurance. No income requirement. Completely free.

---

### They're Confidential

Crisis lines are confidential. The only exception is if you're in immediate danger and request they contact authorities.

**Your privacy is protected.**

---

### You Don't Have to Explain Everything

You don't need to have it all figured out. Just say:

- "I'm in crisis"
- "I'm having suicidal thoughts"
- "I'm struggling"
- "I need to talk to someone"

Counselors are trained to help. You don't need perfect words.

---

### Crisis Lines Help With MORE Than Suicide

You can call or text if:
- Suicidal thoughts
- Self-harm urges
- Severe anxiety or panic
- Feeling overwhelmed or hopeless
- Depression or emotional distress
- Just needing to talk
- Someone else is in crisis (you're worried about them)

---

### They Won't Call Police (Usually)

Crisis lines do NOT automatically call police.

**Police involvement only if:**
- You're in immediate, life-threatening danger AND
- You agree to it (usually) OR
- They believe you pose immediate threat to yourself/others

**This is rare.** Most calls are just counseling.

---

### Text If Phone Is Hard

Many crisis lines offer text options if:
- You can't speak safely
- You have selective mutism
- Speaking is difficult
- You prefer texting

**Text options available in US, Canada, UK, Australia, and many other countries.**

## If Something is Still Hard

### You're Not "Sick Enough" to Call

If you're asking: "Am I sick enough to call?" → **You are.**

Crisis lines don't judge. You don't need a specific diagnosis, proof of disability, or permission.

---

### You're Worried About Wasting Their Time

You're not. Crisis lines exist for exactly this. Calling is not wasting their time.

---

### You're Afraid of What Will Happen

If you call 988 (or equivalent) and just want to talk → they will talk to you. That's it.

Police involvement is rare and usually requires immediate danger + other factors.

---

### You've Called Before and It Didn't Help

Different counselors, different calls. Try again. Or try a different service.

Some calls help a lot. Some help a little. Some just get you through the night. All of that counts.

---

## After the Crisis

### Immediate After

- Rest (if possible)
- Contact a trusted person
- Do something that helped before (if safe)
- Avoid making big decisions right now

### Short-Term Support

- Schedule an appointment with a therapist or counselor
- Ask your doctor for a mental health referral
- Connect with a support group (in-person or online)
- Call crisis line again if needed

**It's okay to need multiple calls.** People use crisis services multiple times. That's normal.

### Finding Ongoing Help

**US:** [[1-800-662-4357|SAMHSA]] (free treatment referrals)  
**Canada:** Talk to your GP or contact provincial mental health services  
**UK:** Ask your GP for NHS mental health services or therapy referral  
**Australia:** Contact your local Primary Health Network or talk to your GP
