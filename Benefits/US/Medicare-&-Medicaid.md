# Medicare (U.S.)

**Medicare** is a federal health insurance program for:

- People **65 and older**, and  
- Many disabled people who qualify through **SSDI** or certain conditions.

It has several parts and often interacts with other programs such as Medicaid.

---

## The Main Parts of Medicare

Very simplified:

- **Part A – Hospital Insurance**  
  Often covers inpatient hospital care, limited skilled nursing, some home health, and hospice.

- **Part B – Medical Insurance**  
  Covers many outpatient services: doctor visits, labs, durable medical equipment, some therapies.

- **Part D – Prescription Drug Coverage**  
  Private plans approved by Medicare that help cover medication costs.

- **Part C – Medicare Advantage**  
  Private plans that bundle Parts A and B (and often D), sometimes with extra benefits, but also their own rules and networks.

(Details and costs vary by plan and change over time.)

---

## How Disabled People Qualify

You may become eligible for Medicare before age 65 if:

- You receive SSDI for a certain period, or  
- You have specific conditions covered under Medicare rules.

See: [/benefits/us-ssdi](/benefits/us-ssdi)

---

## Medicare + Medicaid (“Dual Eligibles”)

If you qualify for **both**, you might:

- Have Medicare as primary coverage, and  
- Have Medicaid help with premiums, co-pays, and services not fully covered by Medicare (like long-term care or additional HCBS).

This can be very complex. Many areas have **special plans** for people who are “dual eligible.”

---

## Common Challenges

- Understanding premiums, deductibles, and co-pays  
- Deciding between **Original Medicare + Part D** vs **Medicare Advantage**  
- Access to specific doctors, hospitals, or medications within a plan’s network  
- Coverage rules for durable medical equipment, home health, or rehab

Because rules and options change regularly, many people:

- Use **SHIP programs** (State Health Insurance Assistance Programs)  
- Talk to local benefits counselors or advocates  
- Check official Medicare resources for current information

---

## Where This Fits in the Benefits Landscape

Medicare often works alongside:

- SSDI (as the gateway for many under-65 disabled people)  
- Medicaid (for dual eligibles)  
- VA health care (for veterans, sometimes combined with Medicare)
