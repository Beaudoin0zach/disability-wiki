# Discord Servers

**What this page covers:** Finding disability communities on Discord, understanding Discord culture, and creating an accessible server.

**Quick find:** Use DISBOARD.org (largest Discord directory). Search by tags: autism, ADHD, chronic-illness, pain, disability. Filter by community type and size.

---

## Why Discord for Disability Communities

**Strengths:**
- Real-time chat enables immediate connection
- Voice channels enable non-typing participation
- Gaming origins = naturally neurodivergent-friendly
- Body doubling for productivity/social support
- Highly customizable and moderable
- Active moderation possible
- Smaller, closer-knit communities typical
- Supports multiple communication styles (text, voice, video)

**Limitations:**
- Requires account creation
- Steeper learning curve than other platforms
- Mobile app less accessible than desktop
- Less anonymous than Reddit
- Smaller average community size
- Quality varies by server

## Understanding Discord Basics

### What Is Discord?

Discord is a chat platform originally designed for gamers. It offers:
- **Text channels** - conversations organized by topic
- **Voice channels** - real-time voice chat, optional video
- **Direct messages** - private conversations
- **Roles** - user permissions/identities in server
- **Bots** - automated tools for moderation, fun, functionality
- **Integration** - connect to other platforms

### Before Joining

**You need:**
- A Discord account (free)
- An email address
- A username

**You don't need:**
- Microphone (text-only fine)
- Webcam
- Gaming experience
- Tech knowledge

### Accessibility Features Built-In

- High contrast mode
- Zoom/UI scaling
- Adjustable text size
- Reduced motion options
- Screen reader support (NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver)
- Keyboard navigation
- Text-to-speech available

## Finding Discord Servers

### Discovery Methods

**DISBOARD.org** (largest Discord directory)
1. Go to disboard.org
2. Search by keyword: [condition name]
3. Filter by tags (autism, ADHD, chronic-illness, disability)
4. Sort by member count, bump time
5. Click server to see description and rules
6. Join button available

**Discodus.com**
- Alternative Discord directory
- Similar search functionality

**Reddit communities**
- Most r/[condition] communities have Discord link
- Search subreddit posts for "Discord"
- Ask in subreddit (many welcome questions)

**Google search**
- "Discord [condition] support server"
- "Discord autism community"
- "Discord ADHD help"

**Twitter/X**
- Follow disability communities
- They often share Discord links
- Use disability hashtags to find communities

**Word of mouth**
- Ask friends with same condition
- Ask in online communities you're already in
- Reddit comment sections often share Discord links

### Evaluating Discord Servers

**Check these things before joining:**

**Description and purpose:**
- Clear statement of server focus
- Who's welcome?
- What happens in this server?
- Any accessibility statement?

**Recent activity:**
- Look at #general or #introductions
- Are posts recent (last week)?
- How many members are active?
- Do moderators post regularly?

**Rules channel:**
- Is there a #rules or #community-guidelines channel?
- Are rules clear and comprehensive?
- Do they address confidentiality?
- Do they mention accessibility?

**Accessibility:**
- Is there accessibility statement pinned?
- Do image posts have descriptions?
- Are there content warnings?
- Is there quiet/low-pressure channel?

**Channels available:**
- Good servers have: welcome, rules, introductions, off-topic, resources
- Avoid: servers with only one or two channels (limited)
- Look for: body double channels, hobby channels, support channels

**Red flags:**
- No recent activity or old messages
- Hostile or mocking tone in messages
- Medical gatekeeping visible
- No rules or confidentiality policy
- Images without descriptions
- No moderation visible

**Green flags:**
- Active, friendly, welcoming messages
- Clear accessibility statement
- Images described
- Content warnings used
- Regular moderation activity
- Diverse members represented
- Regular events or activities

## Discord Culture & Norms

### General Norms

**Text channels organized by purpose**
- #welcome or #introductions: say hi
- #rules: read first
- #general: off-topic chat
- #vent or #support: for difficult emotions
- #resources: community-shared links
- Condition-specific channels: deep dives into your disability

**Roles indicate identity**
- Some servers let you set your own roles (pronouns, disabilities, interests)
- Roles sometimes give channel access (opt-in channels)
- Don't assume anyone's disability, identity, or pronouns

**Emojis have meaning**
- Reactions to posts (👍 agree, ❤️ support, etc.)
- Don't spam rapid-fire emojis (screen reader hostile)
- Emoji-heavy posts can be inaccessible

**Voice channels enable multiple participation styles**
- Text in voice channels if you can't talk
- Mute is normal (talk when you want)
- Video optional
- Lurk is totally fine
- Body doubling (working silently together) common

**Neurodivergent culture**
- Stimming welcomed/celebrated
- Special interests discussed in depth
- Hyperfocus channels common
- "I forgot to eat" understanding laughed through
- Time blindness acknowledged
- Executive dysfunction normalized

### Disability Community Norms

**Confidentiality:**
- What's shared here stays here
- Screenshots are culture-hostile
- Report rule breakers
- Respect privacy

**Peer support, not medical advice:**
- Share lived experience
- No diagnosis gatekeeping
- Self-diagnosis welcomed
- "Check with your doctor" if serious health question

**Accessibility expected:**
- Image descriptions (alt text)
- Video captions required
- Content warnings for difficult topics
- Trigger warnings for trauma
- Clear, accessible language

**Multiply marginalized welcomed:**
- LGBTQ+ affirmed
- BIPOC centered in many servers
- Disabled immigrants understood
- Gender diversity natural
- Class diversity acknowledged

## Discord Accessibility

### What Works Well

**Screen reader support:**
- Channel lists readable
- Message content clear
- Links descriptive
- Roles and permissions navigation

**Keyboard navigation:**
- Tab through channels
- Arrow keys to navigate
- Enter to open/send
- Full keyboard usability

**Visual accessibility:**
- High contrast mode
- Dark mode standard
- Zoom works (Ctrl +/-)
- Font scaling available

**Communication flexibility:**
- Text and voice options
- No requirement to use microphone
- Video optional
- Typing always available

### Limitations & Workarounds

**Mobile app less accessible:**
- Desktop always better for accessibility
- iOS app particular problem
- Android somewhat better
- If on mobile, use browser if possible

**Screen readers:**
- Some buttons unlabeled
- Server menus not fully reachable
- New message notifications inconsistent
- Workaround: Ask moderators for help, use accessible Discord clients if available

**Image accessibility:**
- Discord doesn't require alt text
- Relies on user providing description
- Ask server to enforce alt text policy
- Report inaccessible images to mods

## Joining a Discord Server

### Step 1: Find the Server
Use methods above (DISBOARD, Reddit, Google, etc.). Read description. Check rules and accessibility statement.

### Step 2: Join
Click "Join Server" button. May require accepting rules or completing verification. Some servers have membership screening.

### Step 3: Read Rules & Introductions
- Go to #rules or #community-guidelines (usually first channel)
- Read thoroughly
- Check #introductions to see what people share
- Note any accessibility accommodations available

### Step 4: Introduce Yourself (Optional)
Most servers have introduction channel. Share what you're comfortable with:
- Name/username (can be fake)
- Condition/disability (optional)
- Pronouns (optional)
- Interests (optional)
- Don't overshare if you're not ready

### Step 5: Explore Channels
- Start with text channels (easier than voice)
- Look at pinned messages (important info)
- Join relevant role channels if opt-in available
- Lurk for a bit to understand tone

### Step 6: Participate at Your Level
- Listening is totally valid
- React to posts (emojis)
- Reply to others' posts
- Eventually share your own experience
- Voice channels optional

## Creating a Discord Server

### Before You Start

**Do you really need this?**
- Search thoroughly for existing communities
- Are there similar servers already?
- Will you have co-moderators?
- Can you commit to moderation?
- Do you have a specific, unmet need?

### Creating the Server

**On desktop (recommended for accessibility):**
1. Click + button (left sidebar, bottom)
2. Select "Create My Own"
3. Give it a name (memorable, searchable)
4. Choose region (doesn't matter much)
5. Done! You're in empty server

### Essential Setup (Do These First)

**Enable Community features:**
1. Server Settings → Community
2. Enable Community features
3. Unlocks: rules channel, membership screening, mod tools

**Create essential channels:**

1. **#welcome** - Warm greeting, what server is about, how to get started
2. **#rules** - Clear comprehensive guidelines (see below)
3. **#introductions** - Where people say hi
4. **#faq** - Frequently asked questions
5. **#resources** - Pinned links, crisis hotlines, organizations
6. **#general** - Off-topic chat
7. **#vent** - Designated space for difficult feelings (optional)
8. **#wins** - Celebrate successes (optional)
9. **#accessibility** - Request accommodations (optional)

**Write community guidelines:**

Essential to include:

**Respect & Safety:**
- No discrimination (race, gender, sexuality, disability, class, etc.)
- No harassment or hate speech
- Challenge ableism
- Respectful disagreement welcomed
- No gatekeeping disabilities

**Confidentiality:**
- What's shared stays here
- No screenshots/sharing others' info without permission
- Real names optional

**Peer Support Focus:**
- We are peers, not medical professionals
- Share lived experience, not prescriptions
- Encourage professional help for serious health issues
- No medical gatekeeping

**Accessibility:**
- Alt text required for ALL images
- Captions required for videos
- Content warnings for difficult topics
- Clear, accessible language
- Describe images in text when possible

**Content Guidelines:**
- On-topic: [list what's welcome]
- Off-topic: [list what belongs in #general]
- Vent threads have boundaries
- No unsolicited advice
- No promotion without mod approval

**Consequences for rule violations:**
- First violation: private message explaining
- Second: post removed with explanation
- Third: temporary mute
- Repeated: permanent ban

## Moderation Setup

### Installing Bots

**Dyno** (general moderation)
- Modmail for users to contact mods
- Auto-responses
- Message filtering
- Moderation tools

**MEE6** (similar to Dyno)
- Moderation features
- Auto-mods
- Leveling system (optional)

**Carl-bot** (reaction roles, autoreplies)
- Create roles users click to get
- Auto-replies to common questions
- Tags system

**PluralKit** or **Tupperbox** (for systems/multiple people)
- Enable system members to have own identity
- Front-switching support
- Important for DID/OSDD communities

**Tickets** (accessibility requests, private issues)
- Users create "tickets" for accommodations/concerns
- Private conversation with mods
- Tracks issues

**Mutebot** (content warnings)
- Create spoiler/trigger warnings
- Users opt-in to see content
- Helps with trauma-informed moderation

### Moderation Team Structure

**Lead moderator/admin:**
- Server vision
- Final decisions
- Overall management

**Content moderators:**
- Monitor channels
- Respond to questions
- Facilitate discussions
- ~1 per 50-100 members

**Community moderators:**
- Welcome new members
- Organize events
- Foster connection

**Crisis moderators:**
- Mental health first aid trained
- Handle serious situations
- Refer to professionals

### Moderator Support

- Create private mod channel for discussions
- Hold monthly mod meetings
- Document decisions
- Support moderators' wellbeing
- Cross-train for backup

## Growing Your Server

### Ethical Growth

**Quality over quantity:**
- 50 active members > 500 lurkers
- Create culture first, grow later
- Bad moderation leads to toxic large server

**Promotion strategies:**

- List on DISBOARD.org (free)
- Post in related subreddits (with permission)
- Share on Twitter/Facebook
- Mention in disabilities communities you're in
- Partner with other disability servers
- Create regular engaging events

**Don't oversell:**
- Be honest about server purpose
- Don't recruit universally
- Target specific communities that need this

### Engagement Activities

- Weekly discussion topics
- Monthly movie/game nights
- Member spotlights
- Themed discussion days
- Celebrate milestones
- Regular events on calendar

## Accessibility Planning for Discord Servers

### Visual Design

**Role colors:**
- 4.5:1 contrast ratio minimum
- Avoid red/green only (color-blind accessible)
- High contrast emoji sparingly
- Test with color-blind simulator tools

**Emoji guidelines:**
- CamelCase channel names (#VentSpace not #vent_space)
- Minimize emojis between words
- Rapid-fire emojis = screen reader hostile
- Limit decorative emoji use

**Channel organization:**
- Clear hierarchy
- Easy to navigate
- Sensible naming
- Pinned important info
- Regular cleanup of old channels

### Content Accessibility

**Images:**
- ALWAYS require alt text
- Post description in text form too
- Detailed descriptions (what, who, context)
- Test with screen readers

**Videos:**
- ALWAYS caption
- YouTube auto-captions if nothing else
- Provide transcript when possible
- Manually edited captions better than auto

**Written content:**
- Plain language (avoid jargon)
- Short paragraphs
- Clear structure
- Headings and lists
- Emojis not between words
- CamelCase easier than underscores

**Content warnings:**
- Clear trigger/content warnings
- Allow time before spoiler
- Example: "CW: medical trauma // thread below discusses..."
- Not shame-based ("you probably shouldn't read this")

### Moderation for Accessibility

**Ban behaviors:**
- Unsolicited advice
- Medical gatekeeping
- Ableism unaddressed
- Accessibility hostility

**Enforce consistently:**
- Alt text for all images
- Captions for all videos
- Content warnings for difficult topics
- Respectful communication

## Disability Type Accessibility

**Deaf/Hard of Hearing:**
- Some servers have captions for voice channels (Otter.ai integration)
- Text channels fully accessible
- Request video captions
- Some communities have ASL interpreters available

**Blind/Low Vision:**
- Screen reader support good on Discord
- Desktop better than mobile
- Images need descriptions (beyond just alt text)
- Links should be descriptive

**Neurodivergent:**
- Discord's structure often works well
- Body doubling channels especially valuable
- Special interest deep-dive channels great for hyperfocus
- Low-pressure atmosphere helps with executive dysfunction

**Psychosocial/Mental Health:**
- Crisis resources should be prominently pinned
- Content warnings essential
- Supportive, non-judgmental culture critical
- Mental health disability explicitly welcomed

**Multiply-Marginalized:**
- Seek servers that center your full identity
- LGBTQ+ affirmed explicitly
- BIPOC community welcomed
- Some servers specifically for women, trans people, people of color
