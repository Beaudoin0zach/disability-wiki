# Facebook Communities

**What this page covers:** Finding disability communities on Facebook, evaluating groups, and creating accessible Facebook groups.

**Quick find:** Use Facebook's group search with condition name. Browse "Suggested Groups." Search "[your city] [condition] support group."

---

## Why Facebook for Disability Communities

**Strengths:**
- Largest user base globally (billion+ users)
- Easiest discovery for newcomers
- Robust group features (events, files, fundraising)
- Local community easily found
- Parent/caregiver networks strong
- Event planning & real-world connection
- Less technical barrier than other platforms

**Limitations:**
- Privacy concerns (Meta data practices)
- Algorithm changes affect community visibility
- Less disability-affirming culture on average
- Often more parent-led than peer-led
- Moderation varies widely
- Misinformation easier to spread
- Can feel less "community" more "group chat"

## Finding Facebook Disability Groups

### Search Strategies

**Facebook's built-in search:**
1. In Facebook search bar (top), type: "[condition] support group"
2. Click "Groups" tab to filter for groups only
3. Sort by relevance or member count
4. Look for active groups (recent posts, many members)

**Refined searches:**
- "[Your city] [condition] support group"
- "[State/Province] [condition] disability"
- "[Condition] parents" (if parent-focused)
- "[Condition] community" (broader than "support")

**Browse "Suggested Groups":**
- Facebook suggests groups based on your activity
- Check these (algorithm knows what you engage with)
- May surface smaller, more specialized communities

**External directories:**
- Search Google: "[condition] Facebook group"
- Many disability bloggers maintain lists of 50+ Facebook groups
- Disability organizations often link affiliated Facebook groups
- Reddit communities sometimes share Facebook group links

**Word of mouth:**
- Ask in online communities you're already in
- Ask healthcare providers for referrals
- Ask other disabled people in your life
- Post question in general disability groups ("Any FB communities for [condition]?")

### Evaluating Facebook Groups

**Check these things before joining:**

**Activity level:**
- Scroll recent posts (last week?)
- Is there daily/weekly activity?
- Do people respond to questions?
- When did admin last post?
- Are there spammers or trolls?

**Community tone:**
- Read a few posts and comments
- Is tone supportive or judgmental?
- Do people gatekeep diagnosis ("You're not sick enough")?
- Is there humor? Is it kind or cruel?
- How are questions handled?

**Rules and moderation:**
- Does group have pinned rules?
- Are ableist posts removed quickly?
- Do admins moderate actively?
- What's confidentiality policy?
- How are conflicts handled?

**Representation:**
- Who's represented in discussions?
- Are multiple perspectives welcomed?
- Do you see people like you?
- Is diversity visible?

**Accessibility:**
- Do posts include image descriptions?
- Are trigger/content warnings used?
- Is language clear (not jargon-heavy)?
- Can you participate without extreme effort?

**Red flags:**
- Inactive for months (no engagement)
- Hostile comments left unmoderated
- Spam posts not removed
- Heavy medical gatekeeping
- No confidentiality policy
- Misinformation unchallenged
- Unmoderated ableism
- Aggressive recruitment

**Green flags:**
- Active daily/weekly posts
- Quick, respectful moderation
- Clear rules and confidentiality statement
- Welcoming tone in posts
- Mix of support, resources, social
- Diversity visible
- Regular activities (discussions, challenges, events)
- Admin engagement with members

## Facebook Group Culture & Norms

### General Norms

**Mix of content types:**
- Personal stories and venting
- Resource sharing (links, articles, tips)
- Questions and answers
- Celebrations and wins
- Memes and humor (sometimes)

**Posts visible to all members:**
- Everything shared in group is visible to members
- Files, photos, documents
- Reactions and comments from all members
- Can be screenshot or shared (despite rules)

**Admin posts important info:**
- Pinned posts at top have important info
- Check pinned posts when joining
- Events often posted by admin

**Diversity of member types:**
- People newly diagnosed
- Long-term community members
- Healthcare providers (sometimes)
- Family members (if group allows)
- Mix of engagement levels (lurkers, regular posters, mods)

**Algorithmic visibility:**
- Facebook algorithm affects who sees posts
- Active engagement (likes, comments) boost visibility
- Recent posts more visible than old
- Controversial posts (arguments) get more visibility

### Disability Community Norms

**Confidentiality:**
- What's shared here stays here (should be explicit rule)
- Don't screenshot and share outside group
- Respect members' privacy
- Many groups have specific confidentiality rules

**Peer support, not medical advice:**
- Share lived experience
- No diagnosing or contradicting doctors
- "Talk to your healthcare provider" if serious health issue
- Respect diverse treatment approaches

**Self-diagnosis welcomed** (varies by group):
- Many groups explicitly welcome self-diagnosis
- Acknowledge diagnostic barriers
- No gatekeeping about diagnosis
- Check group rules—varies widely

**Multiply-marginalized welcomed:**
- LGBTQ+ often explicitly welcomed
- BIPOC voices centered (in some groups)
- Immigrant experiences acknowledged (in some)
- Gender diversity celebrated (in some)
- Class diversity acknowledged (varies)

**Parent groups vs. peer-led groups:**
- Some groups parent-led (focus: parenting a disabled child)
- Some groups peer-led (focus: lived experience of disability)
- Some groups mixed
- Important to understand group type before joining

## Facebook's Accessibility Features

### Built-In Features

**Screen reader support:**
- Facebook mostly works with screen readers (NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver)
- Group content accessible
- Navigation works with screen readers

**Keyboard navigation:**
- Tab through posts and comments
- Enter to open/read
- Generally keyboard navigable

**Visual accessibility:**
- Dark mode available
- High contrast mode
- Font size adjustable (browser zoom)
- Video captions (if provided by poster)

**Mobile app:**
- Works on mobile (reasonably accessible)
- iOS and Android
- Smaller screen = harder to navigate

### Limitations

**Alt text:**
- Not required or enforced on Facebook
- Relies on individual posters to add
- Many posts missing descriptions
- Report posts without alt text to admins

**Videos:**
- Auto-captions available (imperfect)
- Many videos not captioned
- No transcript requirement
- Request captions from posters

**Images:**
- Huge accessibility issue in Facebook groups
- Many posts are images without text
- Can't be read by screen readers without alt text
- Ask admins to enforce alt text policy

## Creating Effective Facebook Disability Groups

### Planning Phase

**Define your purpose:**
- What specific need does this fill?
- Who is target audience?
- What makes it unique from existing groups?
- How much time can you realistically commit to moderation?

**Research existing communities:**
- Search thoroughly for similar groups
- Understand what exists
- Identify gaps in services
- Check if gap is real need or just untapped market

**Decide on group type:**
- Public group (anyone can find, see posts)
- Private group (found through search, need approval to join)
- Secret group (invitation-only, hidden from search)

**Recommendation:** Start with Private (balance between accessible and moderated)

### Group Creation

**On Facebook (desktop recommended):**
1. Click your profile picture → Groups
2. Click "Create Group"
3. Enter group name (clear, searchable, includes condition if applicable)
4. Add group description covering:
   - Who the group is for
   - What you'll discuss
   - Group values
   - Posting expectations
5. Choose privacy setting (Public, Private, or Secret)
6. Add group icon and cover photo
7. Invite initial members from your network

**Initial setup:**
- Start with 3-5 trusted core members
- Grow slowly and intentionally
- Quality over quantity
- Build culture before scaling

### Essential Group Settings

**Create comprehensive rules** covering:

**Respectful communication:**
- No ableism, discrimination, hate speech
- Respect chosen names, pronouns, identities
- No unsolicited advice or gatekeeping
- Respectful disagreement welcomed

**Confidentiality:**
- "What's shared here stays here"
- No sharing others' information without consent
- Real names optional—use nicknames
- Group may not be fully confidential (warn members)

**Support focus:**
- Peer support, not professional advice
- "We are not medical professionals"
- No diagnosis gatekeeping
- Self-diagnosis respected (if that's your policy)
- Encourage professional consultation for serious issues

**Content guidelines:**
- On-topic: [list what's welcome]
- Off-topic: [list what belongs elsewhere]
- Trigger/content warnings for difficult topics
- No promotion without mod approval
- Photo/video policies (consent required)

**Privacy protection:**
- Don't screenshot others' posts
- No identifying information
- Report harassment to mods
- Mods should enforce privacy

**Consequences:**
- First violation: Private message
- Second violation: Post removed + explanation
- Third violation: Temporary mute
- Repeated violations: Removal

### Moderation Setup

**Create mod team:**
- Start with 1-2 co-admins (can't do alone)
- At 50 members: recruit 1-2 more
- At 100+ members: aim for 5-7 total
- Ensure diverse team across timezones

**Assign roles:**
- Admin: Final decisions, structure
- Moderators: Daily moderation, engagement
- Specialists: Crisis support, accessibility accommodation (if needed)

**Tools Facebook provides:**
- Comment approval (hold posts for review)
- Word filters (auto-remove certain language)
- Post scheduling
- Polls
- Event creation
- File uploads
- Member roles with different permissions

**Moderation protocols:**
- Check group daily (minimum)
- Respond to questions within 24 hours
- Remove spam immediately
- Address rule violations quickly
- Document decisions
- Communicate transparently

### Creating Group Resources

**Pinned posts should include:**
- Welcome message (warm, clear)
- Rules summary (link to full rules)
- Resources (links, hotlines, organizations)
- How to introduce yourself
- Contact mod if problems

**Group description:**
- Confidentiality commitment
- Who it's for
- What it's about
- Accessibility information
- How often you meet (if scheduled)

**Pinned documents/files:**
- Crisis resources
- Disability organizations
- Local services (if local group)
- FAQ (update as needed)

## Growing Your Facebook Group

### Ethical Growth

**Quality over quantity:**
- 50 active members > 500 lurkers
- Disengaged members hurt community
- Better to stay focused and small
- Can always grow if culture established

**Promotion strategies:**
- Post in related groups (with permission)
- Share on personal social media
- Partner with disability organizations
- Post on relevant subreddits
- Search engine optimization (good naming helps)
- Focus on value, not recruitment

**Don't oversell:**
- Be honest about group purpose
- Don't recruit universally
- Target communities that need this specific group

### Engagement Activities

- Weekly discussion prompts (post specific questions)
- Monthly themed discussions
- Member spotlights (highlight member stories)
- Resource shares (curate good links)
- Celebration posts (member wins, milestones)
- Virtual events (Q&A, movie night, etc.)
- Challenge weeks ("Share your biggest challenge")
- Seasonal posts (holidays, awareness months)

## Accessibility & Inclusion Best Practices

### Proactive Accessibility Statement

**Create pinned post:**

"This group welcomes members of all abilities. We encourage image descriptions, welcome self-diagnosis, and provide accommodations for various communication styles. Reach out to a moderator if you need specific support or accessibility accommodations."

### Creating Accessible Spaces

**Image descriptions (encourage, don't require initially):**
- "What does this image show? Any text? Who's in it?"
- Provide in comment if poster doesn't
- Normalize description as group culture
- Eventually enforce as requirement

**Communication accommodation:**
- Welcome various communication styles
- Respect energy limitations (lurking is valid)
- Offer content warnings
- Welcome self-diagnosis if policy allows
- Provide alternative formats when possible (text versions of graphics, plain text info, etc.)

**Accessibility features Facebook provides:**
- Screen reader support (reasonable on desktop)
- Caption support (requires manual addition)
- High contrast mode
- Keyboard navigation
- Mobile app accessibility

### Common Accessibility Issues in Facebook Groups

**Images without descriptions:**
- Most common accessibility barrier
- Can't be read by screen readers
- Solution: Enforce alt text policy, provide descriptions for old posts

**Walls of text:**
- Hard to read on mobile
- Difficult for neurodivergent readers
- Solution: Encourage short paragraphs, line breaks

**Memes as primary content:**
- If not described, inaccessible
- Solution: Encourage "image ID in comments"

**Videos without captions:**
- Common in Facebook groups
- Solution: Require captions or transcript in comments

**Heavy reliance on Facebook's closed caption feature:**
- Auto-captions often inaccurate
- Solution: Manually edited captions better

## Special Considerations for Disability Groups

### Medical Information Boundaries

**Clear statement:**
"We are not medical professionals. We share lived experience, not medical advice. Always consult with your healthcare provider about medical decisions."

**Medical gatekeeping:**
- Don't allow: "You're not sick enough"
- Don't allow: questioning diagnosis validity
- DO allow: "I also have that condition" type sharing
- DO allow: discussing treatment approaches

### Privacy Protection

**Regular reminders:**
- Don't screenshot posts without permission
- Real names optional
- No identifying information in post titles
- Group may not be fully private (warn members)

**For sensitive posts:**
- Create "closed" discussion threads
- Discuss sensitive topics away from group wall
- Or use "who can see this" privacy setting on posts

### Self-Diagnosis Policy

**Decide in advance:**
- Many disability communities welcome self-diagnosis
- Acknowledge diagnostic barriers
- Some groups require diagnosis (decide if you will)
- State policy clearly in rules

## Moderation Templates

### Welcome Post Example

"Welcome to [Group Name]! We're a supportive community for people with [condition/disability]. Here, you're safe to share, ask questions, and connect with others who understand. Please read our [group rules] and introduce yourself in the [introduction thread]. We use this group as a place where [specific value: confidentiality/support/resource sharing/celebration]."

### Rule Violation Response (Private Message)

"Hi [member], I noticed your post [specific issue]. Our community rules ask that we [specific guideline]. Let's keep this a supportive space for everyone. Feel free to reach out if you have questions!"

### Conflict Resolution

"Thank you both for sharing your perspectives. We have diverse views here, and that's okay. Let's remember our shared values of respect and support. I'm closing comments on this thread, but happy to discuss this further via private message."

## Disability Type Accessibility

**Deaf/Hard of Hearing:**
Facebook is text-based (accessible). Request captions if videos used. Video captions important for Deaf participation.

**Blind/Low Vision:**
Desktop Facebook works well with screen readers. Mobile app less accessible. Alt text critical. Report posts without descriptions to admins.

**Neurodivergent:**
Long posts can be overwhelming. Shorter paragraphs help ADHD readers. Clear structure helpful. Some groups use CamelCase or other accessibility markers.

**Psychosocial/Mental Health:**
Community culture crucial. Content warnings for trauma/loss. Supportive, non-judgmental tone. Crisis resources visible.

**Hidden/Invisible Disabilities:**
Your disability is real. No proof needed. Self-diagnosis welcomed. Your experience is valid.

**Multiply-Marginalized:**
Seek groups that center your full identity, not just disability. BIPOC disability groups, LGBTQ+ disability groups, disabled immigrant groups exist. Search specifically for these.

## Global Perspective

### Facebook Representation

**Facebook dominance varies globally:**
- Extremely dominant in Western countries (US, Europe, Australia)
- Still major in Global South but competes with WhatsApp, Telegram
- International disabled communities growing on Facebook
- Many Global South disability groups exist (search in local language)

**Western group culture:**
- Most established English-language groups on Facebook
- US-centric by default
- Medical model sometimes stronger in parent-led groups

**Global South groups:**
- Growing but often smaller
- More family-centered
- Informal economy discussion less common
- May mix English/local language

**Non-English communities:**
- Spanish-language groups large
- French, Portuguese, other European languages
- Asian language groups (vary by country)
- Search in your language for better results
