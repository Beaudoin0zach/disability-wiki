# Global Crisis Hotlines and Helplines

This page lists **crisis and emotional support lines** from multiple regions.  
Availability, quality, and accessibility vary widely.

> **Important:**  
> Many hotlines are linked to police or involuntary hospitalization.  
> If avoiding those systems is important to you, read the notes and consider peer-run or text/chat options where available.

---

## How to use this page

- Check the **region or country** headings.
- Look for notes on:
  - Languages
  - Text/chat options
  - Deaf/hard-of-hearing access
  - Peer-run vs. clinical services
- When possible, we link to **official directories** that stay more up to date than a static list.

## Peer and community-based support

Where available, we highlight:

- **Warmlines** – lines for emotional support that are not limited to “imminent crisis”
- **Peer-run lines** – staffed by people with lived experience of mental health, disability, or distress
- **Identity-specific support** – for example, LGBTQ+ youth lines, lines for migrants, or lines organized by and for specific racialized communities

These are often safer for disabled, mad, or neurodivergent people who have been harmed by carceral or medical systems.

---

## Limitations and cautions

- This list is **not complete** and may change quickly.
- Some numbers may no longer be active or may be region-locked.
- Experiences vary—some people find these services lifesaving, others find them harmful.

Whenever possible, pair hotline information with **local community resources**: crisis centers, mutual aid groups, disability organizations, and trusted individuals.
